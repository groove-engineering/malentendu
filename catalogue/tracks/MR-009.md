# MR-009 — Jaleyka dans la Serre

- **Fusion:** Samba psychédélique × Electroclash × Synthwave noise (Death Grips)
- **Backend:** Suno
- **Crossing:** multi-atom (samba, electroclash, synthwave-noise-hip-hop, field-recording, koto, vielle-à-roue, jaleyka)
- **Audio:** *(pending)*

## Prompt

```
psychedelic spatial soundscape, field recording textures woven throughout,
polyrhythmic alternation between ternary (6/8 samba swing) and quaternary
(4/4 four-on-the-floor) time signatures, samba percussion interlocking
with koto arpeggios, hurdy-gurdy (vielle à roue) as lead melodic voice
droning and grinding over the polyrhythm, abstract crescendo building
intensely danceable energy, party-restarting momentum,

KICK STRUCTURE: no kick drum on the entire track EXCEPT two windows —
first appearance: 36 beats then disappears, second appearance: returns
2 minutes later and stays from minute 4 to minute 6 only,

female mezzo soprano voice floating in the spatial field, then grizzly
bear howls drenched in cathedral reverb replacing the voice,

intermittent jaleyka (Russian traditional shepherd's flute) theme
recurring throughout — groovy, melodic, folklorique but rhythmically
locked to the samba grid,

TWO INTERLUDES (~50 seconds each): synthwave noise hip-hop à la Death
Grips, heavy swing feel, abrasive distorted 808s, shouted vocals,
glitched synths, noisy and aggressive but swinging hard,

FINAL SECTION: incantations sampling/evoking anti-climate-change
speeches processed through electroclash production — vocodered,
monotone, over crude TR-808 kick and minimal throbbing bass,
confrontational and danceable, the political becomes the groove,

overall: a track that restarts a dead dancefloor, the abstraction
never kills the body, the weirdness serves the swing, every element
exists to make someone move who had stopped moving
```

## Exclude

```
ambient drift without pulse, new age meditation, world music smoothie
that flattens the sources into one exotic texture, clean EDM drops,
generic festival builds, anything that resolves the tension into comfort,
straight grid quantization killing the swing, ironic detachment that
refuses to commit to the groove, psytrance arpeggios, prog rock
self-indulgence, anything too long between moments of physical impact
```

## Structure (indicative)

| Section | Durée approx. | Éléments clés |
|---------|---------------|---------------|
| Ouverture | 0:00–1:30 | Field recordings, koto, vielle à roue drone, pas de kick, samba en 6/8 |
| Premier kick | ~1:30 | Kick entre pendant 36 temps (~30 sec en 6/8) puis disparaît |
| Montée abstraite | 2:00–3:30 | Polyrythmie ternaire/quaternaire, mezzo soprano, jaleyka intermittent |
| Interlude 1 (Death Grips) | ~3:30–4:20 | Synthwave noise hip-hop, swing lourd, ~50 sec |
| Kick retour (min 4–6) | 4:00–6:00 | Kick présent, énergie maximale, hurlements de grizzli + reverb |
| Interlude 2 (Death Grips) | ~5:10–6:00 | Deuxième passage noise hip-hop dans la fenêtre kick |
| Final electroclash | 6:00–fin | Incantations climat, vocoder, TR-808, minimal, dansant, politique |

## Notes de registre

- 🎼 **Musicologique** : polyrythmie 6/8 ↔ 4/4, samba (syncopation, surdo absent remplacé par kick sélectif), koto (gamme pentatonique miyako-bushi), vielle à roue (bourdon + mélodie), jaleyka (échelle diatonique, ornements traditionnels russes), electroclash (120–135 BPM, TR-808, vocoder monotone)
- 👂 **Ressenti** : montée en puissance abstraite et intensément dansante — relancer une fête
- ✊ **Politique** : le final transforme le discours écologiste en matière dansante — la parole militante n'est plus sermon mais groove, forme et non contenu (méta-guardrail §7)
