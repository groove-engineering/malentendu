# The political vision of Le Malentendu

> Register 3. The engine room, not the lyrics — enacted in the crossings, the license, the forgery.

## I. Authenticity is a power relation — and we forge to prove it.

In culture, "the real" is certified by gatekeepers: labels, Discogs, critics, the market. Forging a credible record that enters the register is a demonstration that authenticity is an **effect of certification, not an essence**. We don't attack a record — we attack the **criterion of the real**. (Debord: the spectacle. Benjamin: the aura in the age of reproduction.) — It is said by *existing*; no speech explains it.

## II. Against enclosure, for the commons.

AI models privatize humanity's collective musical culture and resell it. The engine is free (AGPL): a **commons that resists enclosure**. The position: what was made by all must not be re-privatized by a few. (Free-software politics; Lewis Hyde, *The Gift*.)

## III. Creolization, not smoothie.

Crossing genres is two opposite things: **creolization** (Glissant — the unpredictable collision of cultures that produces the genuinely new, a force of liberation) or **flattening** (the market's smoothie, every difference dissolved into frictionless content = slop). The vision is *for* creolization, *against* flattening. Keep the friction (the tension field), keep the human ear, throw out the slop. (Édouard Glissant, *Poetics of Relation*.)

## IV. The right to opacity — against total legibility.

The spectacle, the AI, the database demand that everything be legible, classifiable, indexable, extractable. Glissant: the **right to opacity** — cultures have the right to remain opaque, not entirely knowable, not extractable. Le Malentendu defends opacity by producing the unclassifiable, the forged, the noise the indexing machine cannot digest cleanly. **Illegibility as resistance.** (Glissant; James C. Scott, illegibility against the State.)

## V. There is no clean outside — so the work implicates itself.

The project uses an extractive AI, courts investors, eyes a prestigious conference. It cannot stand outside the spectacle and critique it pure — **there is no exterior** (Debord). Political honesty is to operate *within* the contradiction and make it visible: the forger is in the forgery; the critique of extraction is itself an extraction, named. We refuse the comfort of the pure critic.

## VI. Meaning against content — the human ear as a stance.

The spectacle's end point is infinite frictionless content. To defend the **irreducibly human** — taste, the ear, the gesture, the 2 a.m. surprise — is to affirm that culture is **lived meaning, not extractable throughput**. (The instrument against the factory, already decided.)

## Positive synthesis

> Le Malentendu defends **creolization** and the **right to opacity**, against **enclosure** and **flattening** — with the enemy's weapons, and owning it.
